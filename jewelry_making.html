Jewelry Making

The perfect accessory has the power to instantly transform an outfit! It can dress it up (or down), add a pop of color, or even a bit of whimsy. Making your own jewelry gives you the freedom to create something unique to your own style, for every occassion.

My personal taste in jewelry has always been quite varied. I love adding large, colorful, statement jewelry to an otherwise basic outfit to go from "meh" to "Wow!" or adding a small stud to an already amazing outfit just to polish the look. After years of purchasing my jewelry, I decided to finally put my own abilities to use and create my own!

Here I'll show you just some of the materials and tools I use to complete my pieces.

Findings

These are used to connect beads into a finished jewelry piece, such as: ear wires, head pins, jump rings, crimps, clasps, and spacers. They are available in a range of materials and colors.

Beading Chain, Wire, and String

These are used to string beads together and are available in a variety of materials, colors, gauges, shapes, and strengths.

Tools

There are many tools on the market for jewelry making. The tools that I use most frequently and their uses are shown below:


  • Chain Nose Pliers: Gripping wire or findings, opening/closing jump rings, bending wire
  • Crimper Pliers: Closing the crimp bead and creating round crimp bead edges
  • Flat Nose Pliers: Gripping wire or findings, opening/closing jump rings, closing loops
  • Nylon Jaw Pliers: Smoothing out bends in wire or gripping delicate objects
  • Round Nose Pliers: Creating curved wire bends or rings
  • Side Cutter Pliers: Cutting wire
  • Looper Tool: Creating loops at the end of head or eye pins
